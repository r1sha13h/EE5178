Edge Discontinuity
Since DFT mathematically assumes that your image is one "tile" in an infinitely repeating 2D mosaic.
In real images, the left edge rarely matches the right edge. When the DFT "tiles" the image, a sharp intensity jump (discontinuity) occurs at every boundary.
This manifests as a bright "Spectral Cross" (horizontal and vertical lines) through the center of your frequency plot.
We multiply the image by a Window Function w(x,y) that smoothly tapers the pixel values to zero as they approach the edges. This makes the tiled transition seamless.
