Wrap-around (Ghosting)
Because the DFT assumes the image is periodic, the Convolution Theorem performs Circular Convolution.
If you apply a filter (like a blur) without enough space, pixels that "exit" the right side of the image frame will mathematically "wrap around" and bleed into the left side.
To achieve Linear Convolution (standard filtering), we must pad the image with a border of zeros. This provides a "buffer zone" that prevents the filter from interacting with the periodic copies of the image.
