Discrete Fourier Transform
In the physical world, light is continuous. In CV, we sample that light into pixels. This discretization is what forces us to move from the integral to the sum.
Example: 8-bit depth maps light intensity into integers from 0 (black) to 255 (white)
To mathematically discretize a continuous image f(x, y), we multiply it by a 2D Impulse Train s(x, y), which acts as a sampling grid of Dirac delta functions.
